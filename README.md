## 📌 Executive summary

This project is **not** a grab bag of “social media tips.” It is an **intelligence map**: what ~**10** high-signal **B2B SaaS / GTM** voices do in public, how that connects to **demand**, **positioning**, **distribution**, and **pipeline**, and which **patterns** show up when content is run as a **system** instead of one-off posts.

**Core idea**

> **Organic LinkedIn pays off when it sits inside a broader growth system** — positioning and narrative, demand creation, distribution, and measurement tied to **commercial outcomes**, not reach alone.

**Research objective**

Move past isolated tactics and clarify **how content drives real business**: trust before sales, opportunity quality, go-to-market efficiency, and a coherent story in the market.

## 💡 Key insight

> **Organic LinkedIn is not a standalone marketing tactic — it is a growth asset** when it is integrated with positioning, distribution, and the commercial conversation.

When executed well, it helps:

- **Create and educate demand** before the buyer is in “RFP mode”  
- **Improve pipeline quality** (less noise, more ICP)  
- **Reduce sales friction** (less explaining from zero on every call)  
- **Reinforce positioning** (one story across feed, website, and the room)  
- **Compound over time** (content that **stacks**, not only point-in-time campaigns)

## 👤 How to use this project

- **Founders / CEOs** — Pressure-test whether organic content matches **market thesis** and **commercial efficiency**, not only visibility.  
- **Marketing / content** — Shift from reactive calendars to a **system**: message, cadence, distribution, and KPIs sales actually cares about.  
- **Growth / demand gen** — Tie **demand creation** to **capture** and to what the team actually says on calls.  

**Suggested path (15–20 minutes)**

1. Read [`research/sources.md`](research/sources.md).  
2. Read [`research/other/content-patterns.md`](research/other/content-patterns.md).  
3. Open **one** brief in [`research/linkedin-posts/`](research/linkedin-posts/) for your current bottleneck (e.g. Dunford/Pierri if the story is muddy; Simmonds/Natividad if reach is the gap; Walker if the debate is demand vs. leads).

## ✅ Closing

The companies that **win** on LinkedIn are not the ones that post the most by volume. They are the ones that combine:

- **clear positioning**  
- **trust built through consistency**  
- **intentional distribution**  
- **an explicit link to revenue and pipeline**  

This repository shows **what that looks like in practice**, through operators who already live it in B2B SaaS.
