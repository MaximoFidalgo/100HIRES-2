## Post Analysis (Selected)

### Post 1 — B2B Positioning Shifts (Process, Cadence, Downstream)

- **Link:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aprildunford_consumer-products-rarely-shift-positioning-activity-7179200520924901376-O7GO
- **Format:** Educational / Strategic
- **Core idea:** **Consumer-style positioning thinking breaks in B2B:** consumer brands **rarely** reposition; **B2B tech is supposed to** as the market, competitors, and product move. She argues for **(1) a structured positioning process** (you will repeat it), **(2) scheduled reviews**—she used **six-month** check-ins in-house plus **emergency** sessions on major triggers (new competitor, acquisition, major release)—and **(3) explicit downstream work** when positioning shifts: **sales pitch, messaging, and related enablement** updated **with** the new frame.
- **Why it performs:**
  - Turns positioning from **“brand project”** into **governance** the CEO can recognize.
  - Names **triggers** so teams aren’t surprised when narrative work returns.
- **Deeper executive lens:**
  - **Static positioning** is a hidden tax: **slower deals**, **muddier hiring**, **wasted content**, and **channel conflict** (what the founder says vs. what AEs say).
  - **LinkedIn** is a **high-frequency** surface: without **review discipline**, the feed **fossilizes** a story that **product and competition** have already outgrown.
  - Complements **Pierri** (homepage truth) and **Walker** (demand narrative): both assume the **core story can change**; Dunford specifies **when and how** to manage that change.
- **Business implication:** **Fewer emergency rebrands** and **fewer “we sound like three companies”** moments across web, outbound, and exec voice. 🔄
- **What to copy:** Add **Positioning & narrative** to the **operating rhythm** (quarterly business review or semi-annual strategy week) with **pre-listed triggers** and an **owner** for **sales + marketing + product** alignment.

### Post 2 — Stop Making Buyers Do the Comparison Work

- **Link:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aprildunford_most-companies-position-in-a-way-that-forces-activity-7287091309134127105-5s-H
- **Format:** Educational / Contrarian
- **Core idea:** **Too much “positioning” is only “what we’re good at.”** Buyers are **never** evaluating in a vacuum—they’re comparing you to **today’s approach** and **other options**. A **feature list** or **strength stack** leaves them to infer **uniqueness** and **fit**. **Strong positioning** spells out what they **don’t get elsewhere** and **why that matters for their business**.
- **Why it performs:**
  - One paragraph explains **why great products lose** to **clearer stories**.
  - Unifies **homepage, first call, and LinkedIn** under one failure mode.
- **Deeper executive lens:**
  - This is the **CEO-readable** version of **competitive alternatives + value**—the same spine **Pierri** and **Flanagan** point to in **product and GTM** language.
  - **Natividad / Simmonds** can **distribute** a story fast—but if the story **doesn’t complete the comparison**, you scale **buyer confusion**, not **preference**.
  - **Hormozi-style** clarity: if the prospect can’t repeat **“why you vs. X”** in a sentence, you don’t have positioning—you have **description**.
- **Business implication:** **Higher win rates** where you’re **actually differentiated**; faster **disqualification** where you’re not (which **protects** CAC and CS capacity). 🎯
- **What to copy:** Run every hero line and **exec post** through: **Alternative? Unique value? Best-fit customer? Proof?**—then **publish** only when all four are honest.

### Post 3 — Positioning → Sales Pitch (Designed to Help Buyers Buy)

- **Link:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aprildunford_positioning-salespitch-productmarketing-activity-7046485599754612736-f2Eb
- **Format:** Strategic / Product marketing
- **Core idea:** **Positioning work should finish in translation:** a **sales pitch** distinct from **investor**, **employer**, or **vision** narratives—built to **help buyers buy**: **trade-offs**, **market POV**, and **why you’re the best choice for a defined buyer**—**conversationally**, rejecting **aimless demos**, **generic problem/solution**, and **trend-chasing** pitches.
- **Why it performs:**
  - Answers the CEO question: *“We fixed positioning—why don’t deals feel easier?”*
  - Makes **product marketing** visibly **revenue-adjacent**.
- **Deeper executive lens:**
  - **LinkedIn** becomes the **public chapter** of the same **sales story**: teach **how to choose**, not **how to sit through a tour**.
  - Aligns with **Dave Gerhardt** (brand + narrative), **Kyle Poyar** (packaging and motion), and **Chris Walker** (what the market **believes** before the demo): **one spine**, **many formats**.
  - **Welsh / Flanagan**: cadence and systems **amplify** whatever story you lock—**lock the right one first**.
- **Business implication:** **Shorter consensus cycles** in complex deals; **less “we need another deck”** churn because **narrative and product truth** match. 🗣️
- **What to copy:** Write the **buyer pitch** first; **derive** the **30-day LinkedIn calendar** from its **chapters** (problem frame, alternatives, proof, implementation reality).

## What a CEO Should Take Away

- **Positioning is operating infrastructure**, not a **tagline sprint**—especially in B2B where **reality changes**.
- **If buyers still have to “figure you out,”** you’re paying for that labor in **cycle length and CAC**.
- **The sales pitch is the stress test:** if it doesn’t flow from positioning, **neither will** your **LinkedIn** or your **homepage**.

## Practical Playbook (30-60-90 Day View)

- **Days 1–30: Baseline and triggers**
  - Document **best-fit customer**, **true alternatives** (status quo + vendors), **value**, **category frame**, and **proof**.
  - Publish a **trigger list** (competitor, M&A, major release, **ICP shift**, pricing change) that **forces** a **positioning checkpoint**.
  - Assign **one DRI** for **narrative consistency** across **web, sales, and exec channels**.
- **Days 31–60: Translate to revenue language**
  - Build the **buyer sales narrative** (not the fundraising deck); map **each chapter** to **one LinkedIn theme** per week.
  - Audit **first calls** and **win notes** for **mismatch** vs. public story; fix **top 3 contradictions** first.
- **Days 61–90: Measure and institutionalize**
  - Run **win/loss** on **clarity** (“Could the buyer repeat why us?”).
  - Lock **semi-annual** positioning review (or **quarterly** if velocity is high); **pre-brief** the board on **narrative changes** the same way you brief **forecast** changes.

## KPIs to Track

- **Narrative pull-through:** % of **AEs / SDRs** using the **same “vs. alternative”** frame in recorded calls
- **Buyer clarity:** unsolicited **“why you”** articulation in **discovery** (qual scoring)
- **Commercial:** **win rate**, **cycle length**, **stage conversion** after narrative tightening
- **Marketing efficiency:** **cost per qualified conversation** (not just MQLs) once story sharpens
- **Operational:** **time to update** site + deck + **exec LinkedIn** after a **positioning decision**

## Risks to Manage

- **One-and-done workshops** with no **calendar** or **owner**
- **Feature-first LinkedIn** that repeats **Post 2’s** “buyer does the work” failure mode
- **Split narratives** between **founder**, **product**, and **sales**
- **Category cosplay** without **comparison truth** (**Pierri** anti-patterns)
- **Over-rotating on trends** in the feed while **core positioning** stays fuzzy
